# The LENS — Zambia Untold Product Doctrine

Zambia Untold is a **two-lens platform** for national identity and context: one lens for the past (archive, museum, deep time), one for the present (live systems, observatory). Both use the same globe; the product experience makes the split explicit and keeps the museum first.

---

## 1. What This Is

**A time-navigation system for national identity.**

The thesis line says it:

> “Before there were nations, there was a substrate.”  
> **ZAMBIA UNTOLD**

The platform organizes reality in layers:

| Layer        | Meaning |
|-------------|--------|
| Geological   | Deep earth, copper, rift systems |
| Civilizational | Kingdoms, trade, migrations |
| Colonial    | Extraction imposed on the landscape |
| Sovereign   | Post-1964 self-determination |
| Present     | Infrastructure, satellites, environmental signals |
| Future      | Energy transition, hydrogen, critical minerals |

The timeline and globe together form a **temporal navigation interface**. Users are not only reading history; they are moving through space and time.

---

## 2. The Two Lenses

### Lens 1 — Archive / Museum (Primary)

**Purpose:** Historical grounding, cultural preservation, civilizational narrative.

**This is the default public experience.** First load is museum-first.

**Content and interactions:**

- Deep Time scrubber  
- Historical markers and narratives  
- Folk tales (Inganji)  
- Calendar and world-context events  
- Sovereignty Stack (governance, value, infrastructure by epoch)  
- Village search, community memory (Isibalo)

This is the **digital museum**: the primary way students, teachers, historians, visitors, and ordinary Zambians discover Zambia.

### Lens 2 — Observatory / Live (Second)

**Purpose:** Present-day Zambia as a sensing surface — satellites, environment, and (over time) selected infrastructure and sector signals.

**This is a deliberate second lens.** It is **not** the default. It loads only after explicit user intent (e.g. “Observe the Present” / Second Lens CTA or turning on Space / Earth observation in Map Layers).

**Content and interactions (when enabled):**

- Satellite tracking (NORAD-derived, one truth model)  
- Earth observation (e.g. fires, events)  
- Mission proposals and community submissions (curated)  
- Future: climate, agriculture, infrastructure, etc.

The observatory makes the platform a **national observatory interface** — but it stays secondary to the museum in IA and first-load behavior.

---

## 3. One Globe, Two Modes

Both lenses use the **same globe**. Only the time scale and layer set change:

- **Observe the Past** → Archive lens (default): deep time, markers, stories, sovereignty.  
- **Monitor the Present** → Observatory lens (opt-in): live satellites, EO, and later other live signals.

Entry and IA should make this choice clear (e.g. mode selector or clear “Second Lens” entry), not mix both on first load.

## 4. Who It Serves

| Audience   | Primary need | Primary lens |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Students  | Historical continuity, identity, science | Museum |
| Teachers  | Curriculum tools, visual history | Museum |
| Historians| Curated evidence, citations | Museum |
| Visitors  | National identity, discovery | Museum |
| Citizens  | Village, community memory (Isibalo) | Museum + contribute |
| Diaspora  | Reconnection, heritage | Museum |
| Researchers| Datasets, policy context | Both, as needed |

Different users come for different reasons; the **default** is the museum. The observatory is for those who deliberately choose present-day signals.

---

## 5. Product Principles

- **Museum-first:** Homepage and first load are archive/museum. No eager observatory boot.
- **Observatory by intent:** Live layers and observatory UI appear only after the user opts in (Second Lens, Map Layers, or equivalent).
- **One truth per live category:** e.g. one NORAD-derived source for all public satellite counts and readouts; no conflicting HUD vs globe numbers.
- **Operator vs public:** Moderation, review, and operator tooling are **not** part of the public IA. They live on protected routes or behind explicit operator entry.
- **Sovereign, high-trust:** Stored locally, no external tracking; data presentation and sourcing must be consistent and credible.
- **CopperCloud subtle:** Infrastructure attribution (e.g. “Powered by CopperCloud”) is acceptable; the product is not a CopperCloud marketing surface.

---

## 6. Why This Structure Works

- **Continuity, not only past:** The story is geology → copper → trade → colonial extraction → sovereignty → energy transition. The platform is about continuity, not a frozen “past.”
- **Space + time:** History is a spatial operating system. A student can see Kalambo Falls, copper geology, colonial railways, and modern mining as one system.
- **Sovereign memory:** The goal is to unify fragmented records (colonial archives, academic work, oral tradition, government reports) into a single narrative — an epistemic intervention.

---

## 7. Long-Term Direction: Sovereign Knowledge Engine

If executed well, Zambia Untold becomes:

- A national memory archive  
- A spatial history engine  
- A live observation surface (second lens)  
- A diaspora reconnection portal  
- A research and policy visualization platform  

That combination is rare. The **digital national twin** idea is a useful long-term framing: a sovereign interface to a country’s past, present, and emerging future — geography, history, culture, community memory, and live systems — navigated through space and time.  

The product path is: **museum first, observatory second, same globe.** The twin vision extends from that, not the other way around.

---

## 8. Summary

- **What:** Two-lens platform (archive + observatory) on one globe; time shifts from past to present by user choice.  
- **Default:** Lens 1 (museum/archive). First load is deep time, markers, stories, sovereignty — no eager live stack.  
- **Second lens:** Lens 2 (observatory) only after explicit intent; one truth model per live category; operator tooling off the public IA.  
- **Principle:** “Observe the Past” is the default. “Monitor the Present” is a deliberate second step. Same globe, clear boundaries, high trust.
